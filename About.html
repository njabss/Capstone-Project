Below is the National Anthem of Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso's national anthem is called "Une Seule Nuit" (One Single Night). It was composed by Thomas Sankara, the country's former president, and it reflects the spirit of unity and resilience of the Burkinabé people.

Burkina Faso is a landlocked country located in West Africa. It is bordered by Mali to the northwest, Niger to the northeast, Benin to the southeast, Togo and Ghana to the south, and Côte d'Ivoire to the southwest.

The largest ethnic group in Burkina Faso is the Mossi people, who settled the area in the 11th and 13th centuries. They established powerful kingdoms such as Ouagadougou, Tenkodogo, and Yatenga. In 1896, it was colonized by the French as part of French West Africa; in 1958, Upper Volta became a self-governing colony within the French Community.

Flag of Burkina Faso


Burkina Faso gained independence from France on August 5, 1960. The country was initially named Upper Volta but was renamed Burkina Faso in 1984 by then-President Thomas Sankara, which means "Land of Honest People" in the local Mossi language.

Burkina Faso is known for its diverse cultures, with over 60 different ethinc groups, each with its own language and traditions. The country is also famous for its vibrant music and arts scene, particularly the internationally recognized FESPACO film festival held in Ouagadougou.

The Mossi people make up 52% of the population and this is excluding the other 60 ethnic groups which constitute 48% of the population.
